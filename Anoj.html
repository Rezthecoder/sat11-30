Festivals of Nepal

Dashain

Dashain is the biggest and longest festival in Nepal, symbolizing the victory of good over evil. Families gather to celebrate with blessings, feasts, and flying kites.

Tihar (Deepawali)

Tihar is the festival of lights, honoring animals like crows, dogs, and cows. It celebrates the bond between brothers and sisters with Bhai Tika.

Holi

Known as the festival of colors, Holi is full of joy, where people smear colored powder and splash water to celebrate the arrival of spring.

Maghe Sankranti

This marks the beginning of the holy month of Magh. People eat sesame sweets, ghee, and yams, and take ritual baths in rivers for good health.

Indra Jatra

Indra Jatra is celebrated in Kathmandu with mask dances and chariot processions. It honors the god Indra and the living goddess Kumari.

Different Castes & Cultures of Nepal

Tamang

The Tamang are one of the indigenous ethnic groups of Nepal, known for their unique language, traditions, and festivals like Lhosar.

Newar

Newars are the historical inhabitants of the Kathmandu Valley, famous for their rich culture, cuisine, and vibrant festivals such as Indra Jatra.

Magar

Magars are one of the largest ethnic groups in Nepal, known for their bravery and unique festivals like Bhume Puja.

Rai

The Rai community has a rich tradition of oral history, dances, and festivals like Sakela, which celebrate nature and ancestors.

Gurung

Gurungs are renowned for their traditional music, dances, and festivals like Tamu Lhosar that mark their new year.

Tharu

The Tharu people inhabit the Terai region and are known for their colorful attire, dances, and celebrations like Maghi festival.
